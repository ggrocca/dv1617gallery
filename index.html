Hall of Fame

Data Visualization 2016–2017



This page showcases the best projects developed by students of the Data Visualization course, academic year 2016/2017. The course is part of the M.Sc. program in Computer Science offered by DIBRIS (University of Genova).

Teachers: Prof. Enrico Puppo and Dr. Luigi Rocca.

1. C. Accinelli, S. Minisi: A visualization tool for the Africa Leadership Change (ALC) dataset. Publication of this project is currently on hold due to a confidentiality agreement.
A comprehensive visualization of political events in Africa, with a map, a timeline, and additional socioeconomic variables. The dataset was kindly provided by Prof. G. Carbone and Dr. A. Pellegata (see their journal article Researching the Dynamics of Leaders' Replacement: The Africa Leadership Change (ALC) Dataset, and their forthcoming book Political Leadership in Africa. A Comparative Study of Sub-Saharan Leaders. Cambridge University Press, expected 2018).

2. M. Grasso, N. Pinna: Which European country is the most convenient for my job?
Monthly earnings and worked hours in Europe according to occupation type, age and sex. Datasets donwloaded from Eurostat on a suggestion by Dr. Marcello Natili.

3. F. Bozzini, F. Semprini: The Italian Job Market. Gender Balance (1959–2015) and regional hirings (2013,2014,2015).
Original data sources: ISTAT (Italian statistical institute) and INPS (Italian social security agency). We thank Dr. Cesare Benzi for the data elaboration, and for his suggestions and help.

4. P. Corsi, E. Nicora: Journey to the Solar System.
Sizes, distances, temperatures and chemical composition of the planets in the solar system. Data from Wikidata.

5. E. Barelli, F. Marazza: National and regional changes in the Italian job market (2014,2015,2016), and national unemployment trends (2004–2017).
Original data sources: ISTAT (Italian statistical institute) and INPS (Italian social security agency). We thank Dr. Cesare Benzi for the data elaboration, and for his suggestions and help.

6. V. Cincotta, A. Anfigeno: Exploring the effects of the Jobs Act law (2013–2016), and changes in regional job markets (2013–2016).
Original data sources: ISTAT (Italian statistical institute) and INPS (Italian social security agency). We thank Dr. Cesare Benzi for the data elaboration, and for his suggestions and help.
